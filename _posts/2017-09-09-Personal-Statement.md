I was interested in the sky when I was a child, intriguing by the dark and nebulous cosmic, from that time, I have decided to become an astronomer to explore this mysterious world. After growing up, without a doubt, I devote myself to the study of physics in the Department of Physics of Huazhong University of Science and Technology.
![]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/img/64344842_p0.png)
 <!--more-->

During the university, I learned the classical methods like classical mechanic, theoretical mechanic, electromagnetism, atomic physics, thermodynamics, optics, special relativity and quantum mechanic and also the basic mathematics as calculous, linear algebra and matrix theory. Though my GPA may not be so high, it can’t represent my academic performance is not good. I’ve been working on the calculation of the relativistic Fe K shell emission line profiles and disk images from the inner region of an accretion disk around a supermassive black hole(SMBH) and analyzing the data that used to detect GW from pulsar timing arrays under the supervision of Prof. Yan Wang. Such method was published on the Journal of Physics about the LISA symposium XI. Besides, I took part in The University Physics Competition in my junior year though didn’t get a good grade, it was a precious experience in my college life. During my extracurricular, I self-learned Matlab and Mathematica to handle problems, on the other hand, I also learnt C++, Fortran and Python to program so that to simulate  the physical process. In last summer, I went to Berkeley University of California for summer session. When I was there, I took the quantum mechanic class and finally I got a B contributing to my first abroad, which was a great pity for me not getting an A.

After graduate, I went to the KIAA(Kevil Institute of Astronomy & Astrophysics) for my intern under the supervision of K.J Lee in Peking University. During this time, I attempted to find a calculate a theoretical minimum error for the dispersion measure(DM), I have got the mathematical formula but I met some problem when figuring the multiple Gauss distribution. Then I went to the Yunnan Observatories with my two senior colleagues to test the machine built by themselves called ‘Bear’ by my supervisor, a FRB(Fast Ray Burst) searching machine.
